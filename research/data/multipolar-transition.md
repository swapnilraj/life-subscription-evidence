# Comparative Baseline (Macro): Productive Capacity vs Rentier Drift

**Research compiled:** 2026-02-13  
**Standard:** Primary/official sources only (no aggregator or tertiary citations)  
**Purpose:** Benchmark whether major economies are allocating more toward productive capacity (capital formation, manufacturing, R&D, education) or toward financial depth/rentier channels.

---

## Method and Scope

- This document keeps only claims that can be tied to official statistical systems.
- Most cross-country metrics come from the World Bank indicator API (which compiles national statistical sources).
- EU housing-adjacent context uses Eurostat API endpoints directly.
- Indicators have different publication lags (for example, 2024 for GFCF but 2021-2023 for some manufacturing/R&D series).
- Reproducible metric snapshot is generated by `code/build_comparative_primary_metrics.py` into `research/data/provenance/comparative_primary_metrics.json`.

## Bottom Line for the Essay

- The productive-capacity metrics (GFCF, manufacturing share, R&D) support using a **multipolar baseline** rather than a purely Atlantic baseline.
- The UK remains in the lower-investment/lower-manufacturing end of this comparison.
- Claims are limited to what these indicators can support; no intent claims are made from macro aggregates alone.

## Data Quality and Limits

- Indicators are harmonized but not perfectly identical in underlying national methods.
- Publication years differ by indicator and country; each table includes the latest available year.
- “Building vs extracting” is an interpretive frame layered on top of official indicators; the indicators themselves are descriptive.
