# Quick Stats Reference: UK Rent vs Ownership

This file is a writer-facing “one-liner” sheet. Every stat below is either (a) an official published figure with a link, or (b) a model output that is explicitly labeled as such.

---

## Observed (Official)

- **Median total household wealth by tenure (GB, Apr 2020–Mar 2022):** owned outright £647,400; mortgage £404,700; rented £40,800.[^ons_total_wealth]
- **Housing cost burden (England, 2023-24):** private renters spend 34% of income on rent (avg); mortgage holders spend 19% of income on mortgage payments (avg).[^ehs_housing_crisis]
- **Average private rent level (GB, Mar 2024):** £1,246.[^ons_rent_level]
- **Young adult homeownership decline (1996→2015):** age 25-29 55%→30%; age 30-34 68%→46%.[^ons_young_adults]
- **First-time buyers (UK, 2024; Halifax):** average age 33; average deposit £61,090 (~20%).[^halifax_ftb]
- **Local Housing Allowance:** based on a local list of rents at the 30th percentile.[^lha_30th]

## Modeled (Illustrative; Not A Forecast)

**30-year rent-vs-own model (2024→2054) outputs:**[^model]
- **Wealth gap at age 55:** £1,815,516
- **“Dead money” rent paid (30 years):** £845,782
- **Buyer total paid (30 years):** £828,841
- **Renter pays more than buyer (in this scenario):** ~£16,941
- **Final monthly rent at age 55:** £3,885.84
- **Net worth ratio (buyer:renter at 55):** 18.7:1

**Key milestones (model):**[^model]
- Age 30: buyer £184,282 vs renter £91,570 (gap £92,713)
- Age 40: buyer £588,343 vs renter £135,997 (gap £452,346)
- Age 55: buyer £1,918,166 vs renter £102,650 (gap £1,815,516)

**Model assumptions:** see `code/rent_vs_own.py`.[^model]
