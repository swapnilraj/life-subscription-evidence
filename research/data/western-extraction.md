# Comparative Baseline: Extraction Patterns in High-Income Economies (Primary-Data Version)

**Research compiled:** 2026-02-13  
**Standard:** Primary/official sources only  
**Purpose:** Test whether the UK-style “ownership-to-recurring-obligation” story appears in peer economies using measurable, institution-grade indicators.

---

## Method and Guardrails

- This file includes only claims tied to official statistical agencies or central-bank/public-institution releases.
- High-level framing terms (for example, “extraction”) are interpretive and kept separate from the raw metrics.
- Where series are discontinued or lagged, that is stated explicitly.
- Reproducible metric snapshot is generated by `code/build_comparative_primary_metrics.py` into `research/data/provenance/comparative_primary_metrics.json`.

## 2) Medical Debt and Forgone Care (US)

**Primary source:** Federal Reserve, *Economic Well-Being of U.S. Households in 2023 – Expenses* (published 2024)

Reported findings for 2023:
- 23% of adults had major, unexpected medical expenses in the prior 12 months.
- 17% had debt from their own medical care or that of a family member.
- 27% went without some form of medical care because they could not afford it.

**Reading:** The financing layer itself becomes a household burden channel, not just the care price level.

## 3) Student Debt Load (US)

### Household survey evidence (Federal Reserve)
**Primary source:** Federal Reserve, *Economic Well-Being of U.S. Households in 2024 – Higher Education and Student Loans* (published 2025)

- More than 4 in 10 people who pursued education beyond high school reported taking student loans.
- This corresponds to 30% of all adults.
- Median outstanding education debt among borrowers with debt was in the `$20,000-$24,999` bracket.

### System-level stock (Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis)
**Series:** `SLOAS` (Student Loans Owned and Securitized, discontinued)

- Latest observation: Q4 2024 = `1,777,101.97` million USD (~`$1.78T`).
- Series is marked discontinued; latest available value is therefore a fixed historical anchor rather than a live flow.

**Reading:** Education financing remains a large recurring-claims channel on household cash flow.

## Bottom Line for the Essay

- The UK pattern is not isolated: peers also show strong recurring household obligations via healthcare financing, education debt, and housing costs.
- Cross-country comparability is strongest for harmonized indicators (World Bank/Eurostat) and weaker for institutional design details.
- Use this module for comparative context, not for country-specific causal claims without local institutional evidence.
